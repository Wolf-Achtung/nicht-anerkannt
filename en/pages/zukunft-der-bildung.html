Essay

Education After the End of Knowledge Scarcity

Education stands at a historic threshold. For centuries, its institutional form was shaped by a paradigm of knowledge scarcity. Knowledge had to be collected, preserved, structured, transmitted, and reproduced. Teachers were bearers and curators of knowledge, learners their acquirers.

With the entry of generative AI into nearly all contexts of knowledge and creation, this paradigm loses its central position. Systems like GPT, Claude, or Gemini already operate with remarkable precision at the level of symbolic condensation, variant generation, pattern recognition, and linguistic explication.

The task of education shifts radically. Its core is no longer the reproduction of Thirdness, but the cultivation of Firstness.

Firstness Competence as a New Foundation

Firstness designates the qualitative presence of the not-yet-determined: wonder, irritation, pre-propositional perception, the still-unnamed difference. It is that sphere in which something "stands out" before being translated into concepts, methods, or optimization logics.

In the AI age, precisely this capacity becomes the decisive competence for the future:

Firstness competence is the ability to dwell in the undetermined without prematurely closing it conceptually.

Aesthetic SensitivityPerception before concept, resonance before category.
Ambiguity ToleranceEnduring productive emptiness and polysemy.
Abductive InsightGenuine question formation and hypothetical thinking.
Bodily ResonanceEthical sensitivity and the capacity for wonder.
The Anthropological Urgency

This is not merely about new university pedagogy, but about the future of the human being as a cultural creature. The real danger of the AI age lies not in the machine, but in a pedagogy that trains humans to make themselves machine-compatible.

The Danger

Instant answer instead of patient question. Output instead of experience. Optimization instead of perception. Convergence instead of exploration.

The Consequence

Humans who only react probabilistically, surrendering their own Firstness. Education would then have failed its humanistic mission.

The Task

Teaching must become a biophilic sanctuary of the living -- the love of what is becoming, what is open, what is surprising.

Biosemiotics and Negentropy as Educational Foundation

Novelty does not arise from mere complexity, but from living negentropy: from that homeostatic, bodily-grounded form of existence that has something to lose. In this perspective, education is not primarily information processing, but part of the negentropic project of life.

Learning then means not filling a storage device, but cultivating the capacity for resonance:

Perceiving DifferenceRecognizing irritation before it is explained away.
Experiencing ResistanceFriction as a source of genuine insight.
Habit FormationTransforming irritation into hypotheses.
EmergenceNew orders arising from living semiosis.
The Atelier as Epistemic Future Form

The institutional form that corresponds to this insight is the hybrid atelier. The atelier is not merely a production space, but an epistemic architecture in which Firstness is protected and transformed into Thirdness. Here, blurriness may remain productive. Here, the not-yet-sayable is given an initial form through sketches, models, conversation, material, and AI.

Learning begins with irritation, not with instruction.

The atelier organizes learning processes as a continuum of perception, irritation, cut, externalization, critique, feedback, and reformation. This applies to communication design as much as to theory, research, and social responsibility.

AI as Partner of Thirdness

Generative AI receives a clear, non-defensive role in this concept. It takes on structuring, research, condensation, variant generation, perspective shifts, transfer, linguistic explication, simulation of counter-positions, and iteration acceleration.

This does not mean delegating thinking, but outsourcing symbolic labor to strengthen the human focus on Firstness, judgment, and responsibility.

Firstness

The human cultivates wonder, irritation, aesthetic sensitivity, and genuine question formation.

Secondness

The dialogue between human and AI generates productive resistance, friction, and feedback.

Thirdness

AI amplifies regularity, convention, semantic structure, and deductive-abductive recombination.

Didactic Methods

From this perspective, concrete methods emerge that are deliberately designed not to skip over Firstness:

Paradoxical AssignmentsWicked problems and open research questions.
Rapid SketchingPhemic sheets and dialogical critique formats.
Co-Creation SprintsCollaborative work with AI as an instrument of Thirdness.
Productive FailureIterative reflection loops following Manu Kapur.

These methods are not a tool collection, but an epistemic architecture that connects material and media experiments, synchronous and asynchronous atelier dialogues, and collaborative boards.

Assessment Formats of the Future

Assessment must also change. The focus is no longer on the mere correctness of answers, but on:

Quality of Question FormationOriginality of abductive hypotheses.
Documented IterationsReflective capacity and process-oriented learning.
Ethical FramingSocial responsibility and transfer to real contexts.
Working with AIConsciously and reflectively using AI as an instrument of Thirdness.

Assessment thus becomes more strongly oriented toward process, atelier work, and portfolio.

Education as a Humanistic Imperative

This teaching concept is urgent because it determines whether education remains a humanistic project. The future of humanity depends on whether people learn:

To See Before NamingPerception precedes the concept.
To Feel Before CalculatingBodily resonance before algorithmic optimization.
To Ask Before OptimizingGenuine questions instead of efficient answers.
To Design Before DecidingTo enter into relation before functionalizing.

Only thus does culture remain alive. Only thus does design remain research. Only thus does the university remain a place of becoming. And only thus does humanity remain human.
